About Me​

I completed a year of postgraduate training in Somatic Psychotherapy and another year of postgraduate training in Expressive Arts Therapy where I studied directly with the field’s groundbreaking founder, Paolo Knill, at the European Graduate School. My masters degree from Goddard College focused on how systems of social hierarchy affect the neurophysiological substrates of one’s ability to know what they know, feel what they feel, and to express that within a democracy. At the heart of this study was recognition of the revolutionary power of pleasure and love as neurophysiological states that have the power to shift both social and intellectual habits that aren’t inline with our society’s democratic ideals of equality across gender, race, class and all our beautiful subdivisions as folks. My thesis advisor was the wonderful neuro philosopher and artist Ellie Epp, who earned her PhD at Simon-Fraser University. I hold a BA in Choral Directing and Literature from Marlboro College where I graduated with highest honors. I’ve taught workshops in cultural competency as a micro-fusion dance instructor and a choral instructor through Recess Productions.


In my personal time I am an artist and move through the world as a highly sensitive person, with an advanced ability to verbally articulate the HSP experience through phenomenology and neurophysiological studies.

I was raised in rural New Hampshire where I was surrounded and enriched by nature, but challenged by the intergenerational and structural traumas that go along with extreme rural poverty. I have worked with dedication against great odds to excel in each phase of training that have led me to excel in this final phase as an intersectional Rosen Method Practitioner Intern. This path I traveled to begin my private practice has burned into me a blunt and heartfelt awareness of how intersectional experiences of privilege and oppression enter the body-mind as chronic muscular tension, and how to heal effectively at these edges.

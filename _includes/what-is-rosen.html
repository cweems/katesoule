What is the Rosen Method​

Rosen Method bodywork was developed by Marion Rosen, a physical therapist who completed early training in massage therapy and breathwork in Germany during WWII. After many decades of private practice as a physical therapist in the United States, Marion began giving sessions from her home that integrated her early training in massage and breathwork. At the core of her method was the understanding that the psyche is embodied and can be met and healed through touch and breath awareness. Word quickly spread about the amazing results Marion’s clients experienced. Soon she had more clients than she could personally see; she trained students to replicate her work. The results were so successful that now Rosen Method training programs can be found all over the globe.


A private Rosen session lasts fifty minutes and occurs on a massage table draped with sheets. Clothing down to underwear is optional. The practitioner uses a gentle, emotionally present touch to contact areas of the client’s chronic muscular tension. Guided by touch and some verbal dialogue, the client relaxes on the table and experiences how chronic states of emotional and psychological tension are stored in the muscles. Clients who have experienced trauma sometimes have their first Rosen sessions while seated upright in a chair, fully clothed. A client’s boundaries are always honored and we begin our work together from wherever that point of boundary is needed.


Sessions often result in the client feeling a greater sense of vitality, focus, clarity and life purpose, and there is more room for breath to move through their torso. After a Rosen session the client’s social relationships often gradually shift in a positive direction. People react to the client differently after the session than they had before.
